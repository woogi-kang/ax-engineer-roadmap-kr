# Code of Conduct

This project aims to be a collaborative space that respects different levels of experience, industries, and technology choices.

## Expected behavior

- Connect claims and counterarguments to evidence rather than to the person making them.
- Distinguish verified facts, observations from experience, and untested hypotheses.
- Do not belittle beginner questions; provide the context needed to understand them.
- Do not present one technology choice or company experience as a universal answer.
- Remove confidential information about organizations, customers, and colleagues when sharing cases.
- When you find an error, offer actionable evidence before assigning blame.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, insults, or doxxing
- Publishing confidential company information, personal data, or credentials
- Presenting unverified impact or experience as fact
- Repeated advertising, recruiting promotion, or paid-service sales
- Reproducing material while hiding its source

## Reporting

For issues that are not appropriate for a public issue, use a contact method published on the repository owner's GitHub profile. Maintainers may remove content, restrict participation, or adjust visibility when necessary.

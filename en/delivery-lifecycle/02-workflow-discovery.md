# 2. Workflow Discovery

## Purpose

Observe how work actually starts and finishes—including handoffs, waiting, and exceptions—not only the documented procedure.

## Core questions

- What triggers the workflow?
- Who decides what, using which information?
- Is the same information copied or checked repeatedly?
- Where does work wait the longest?
- Does the official procedure differ from practice?
- Who receives exceptions?

## Key activities

1. Interview or observe the actual sequence from trigger to outcome.
2. Record each step's input, action, decision, output, and owner.
3. Mark waiting, re-entry, duplicate checks, omissions, and informal tools.
4. Separate the normal flow from frequent exception flows.
5. Observe how the output is used by the next department or system.
6. Compare current metrics and records with the actual flow.

## Deliverables

- Current workflow map
- Role, system, and data map
- Bottleneck and exception list
- Handoff-loss list
- Open questions

## Exit criteria

- Practitioners can review the map and correct missing steps and exceptions.
- Each step connects an owner with its source of truth.
- No step hides internal judgment behind phrases such as “AI handles it.”
- Every bottleneck is backed by evidence of time, error, waiting, or risk.

## Stop signals

- The flow is finalized from a manager's description without meeting practitioners.
- The official manual is treated as identical to actual work.
- Exceptions are the majority, but only the normal flow is automated.
- The start and end of the workflow cannot be defined.

## Practice

Draw the current flow in Mermaid and ask one practitioner to mark incorrect order, missing exceptions, and unused steps.
